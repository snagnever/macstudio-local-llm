Accuracy — 100-question benchmarks

Pass rate across reasoning/coding/knowledge suites. Phase 1 (Qwen ×3) complete; Phase 2 (Gemma 4) — 26B-A4B@4bit/@6bit complete, 31B-dense partial (MATH/DROP/GPQA still running). LiveCodeBench v6 — local (n=50)

All 7 daily-driver candidates fully characterized after Phase 1 backfill + Step B reruns (2026-05-24). Gemma 4 26B-A4B @6bit at 80 % is the rig's LCB ceiling, +18 pp over the best Qwen (27b at 62 %). Numbers are post-rerun MERGED canonical: qwen/qwen3-coder-next 56 % clean (0 trunc), qwen3.6-27b 62 % (1 trunc, Q3 abc365_c — hardest across the trio), qwen3.6-35b-a3b@6bit 54 % (6 trunc, all 65 k spirals), gemma-4-26b-a4b@4bit 66 % (8 trunc — Q2 recovered at 65 k, rest are real model limits), gemma-4-26b-a4b@6bit 80 % (1 trunc — Q19 still spirals at 65 k).

Terminal-Bench 2.0 — local (n=89, Phase A)

Multi-turn shell-agent benchmark, measured on-rig 2026-05-24 → 2026-05-26 via Harbor + LiteLLM → LM Studio (Docker linux/amd64), terminus-2 agent, concurrency=1, vanilla T-Bench 2.0 task set with --agent-timeout-multiplier 0.5 (bounds the 14 tasks declaring >60-min agent budgets — scores are a defensible floor; full-budget would land ≤5 pp higher). Phase A complete for 3 / 7 candidates: qwen/qwen3-coder-next 32.6 % (29/89, 16.8 h wall-clock), gemma-4-26b-a4b@6bit 21.3 % (19/89, 14.4 h), gemma-4-e4b 4.5 % (4/89, 6.4 h). gemma-4-26b-a4b@4bit still running at snapshot time (2/89 completed, both errored — bar omitted). Phase B (qwen3.6-27b, qwen3.6-35b-a3b, gemma-4-31b-dense) pending. Local vs frontier — published scores

Local runs (this rig, n=100, seed=42, temp=0) plotted against published first-party scores for frontier & notable open-weight models. Caveats: many newer frontier models no longer publish HumanEval/DROP; MATH often means MATH-500 or AIME 2025 (noted per-model); GPQA is Diamond throughout. Reasoning models (o3, R1, Claude 4.x with thinking, Gemini 2.5/3, GPT-5) report with test-time compute. Use for ranking, not absolute deltas — ±5pp noise at n=100.

MMLU

General knowledge. Anthropic 4.x reports MMMLU (multilingual) — marked *

HumanEval

Python coding. Largely deprecated by frontier providers post-2024.

MATH

Original MATH for local/legacy; MATH-500 for DeepSeek (†); AIME 2025 for frontier (‡).

DROP (F1)

Reading comprehension. Mostly dropped by frontier providers — small field.

GPQA Diamond

Graduate-level science. The one benchmark still universally reported.

Quantization caveats — how the on-disk quant compares to the published base

All charts above are base-weight numbers. These are the expected deltas for the user's actual on-disk quants, per community measurements.

  • qwen3-coder-next (MLX 6-bit): essentially lossless. Awni Hannun's Qwen3 perplexity sweep + Unsloth's Q4_K_M analysis show ≥99% retention of BF16 SWE-bench/HumanEval at Q4-class; 6-bit lands within ≤1pt of base.
  • qwen3.6-27b (MLX 6-bit): tracks card numbers within ~0.5pt. Mixed-precision oQ6 vs RTN-6bit shows ~13% lower mean KLD but both are excellent.
  • qwen3.6-35b-a3b (MLX 6-bit): ≤1pt loss on static evals. But documented MoE-MLX issue: multi-turn tool-calling degrades on mlx-community 4-bit/8-bit Qwen3.x-A3B checkpoints (not yet seen on 6-bit; tool-heavy workloads may be more fragile than static evals suggest).
  • ⚠ gemma-4-26b-a4b (MLX 4-bit): the weakest quant on disk. Uniform MLX 4-bit RTN runs ~4.7× worse perplexity than llama.cpp K-quants for Gemma 4. Expect 1–3pt loss on MMLU-Pro/GPQA, 3–5pt on hard math/code. Recommendation: switch to GGUF Q4_K_M or MLX-DWQ-4bit. On-rig measurement (2026-05-21, n=100): the 4-bit vs 6-bit A/B on this rig confirms the penalty — MATH 80 vs 83, GPQA 47 vs 53 (+6pt), DROP unchanged at 79, HumanEval 98 vs 97. The 6-bit lift on GPQA is the biggest single quant-driven score swing observed in Phase 2.
  • gemma-4-26b-a4b (MLX 6-bit): the recommended quant for this MoE on this rig — sits ~1pt under the BF16 published numbers on most evals, beats 4-bit on every reasoning bench, and still runs at ~85 t/s generation. Tool-calling is essentially lossless vs 4-bit (jdhodges 97.5/40, veerman 83.3/12 on both quants).
  • gemma-4-31b (MLX 8-bit, dense): Phase 2 complete (2026-05-22, n=100). HumanEval 95, MMLU 77, MATH 79, DROP 85, GPQA 48, LiveCodeBench 76. The 8-bit dense **ties or loses** to 26B-A4B@6bit on every bench except DROP (+6pt). Decode 13.7 t/s vs @6bit's 80.8 — 6× wall-clock for indistinguishable quality. Verdict: demote / skip in normal rotation.
  • nemotron-3-nano-omni (GGUF Q4_K_M): NVIDIA publishes BF16 → FP8 → NVFP4 deltas (mean of 9 multimodal benches: 65.80 → 65.40 → 65.43). Q4_K_M typically sits between FP8 and FP4 — expect ≤1pt loss on most non-ASR multimodal tasks; ASR (WER) essentially unchanged.
  • gemma-4-e4b (MLX 8-bit, 4B): Phase 2 complete (2026-05-22, n=100). HumanEval 91, MMLU 65, MATH 14, DROP 65, GPQA 34, LiveCodeBench 68, jdhodges 88, Veerman 67. MATH collapses to 14 % — 4B is too small for hard symbolic math. Useful as a FIM / quick-call slot only; not a daily-driver fallback.
  • ⚠⚠ deepseek-v4-flash-dq (MLX 2-bit DQ): heaviest asterisk. V4 Flash ships natively at FP4+FP8 — dropping experts to 2-bit is destructive recompression of an already-quantized model, not a clean post-train quant. The mlx-community card explicitly warns Q4_K_M is the floor for "most of the source quality". No public benchmark deltas exist. Expect 3–8pt drop on hard reasoning, more on long-context. Treat the chart numbers as a ceiling.
  • ⛔ MiniMax-M2.5-3bit (MLX 3-bit) — NO-GO: loads and generates coherently with strong quality (tool-calling 97.5%, HumanEval 95.8%, LCB ~68–74% — all partial), but reproducibly kernel-panics the Mac Studio under sustained inference (Apple GPU driver IOGPUGroupMemory / AGXG16X, macOS 25D125) — three times, independent of parallelism, context, memory pressure, and KV-cache quantization. Not deployable on this stack; only an OS/MLX update or a different runtime (GGUF/llama.cpp) could revisit it. Excluded from the bars (no complete, comparable data). Full detail in tools/local-llm-bench-m4-32gb/results/M4_MAX_128GB_NOTES.md.
